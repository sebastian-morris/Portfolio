Tesla Cyber truck CAD project

A CAD exercise translating a sharply defined vehicle form into controlled geometry, clean surfaces, and presentation-ready output.

Overview

The project explored how a recognizable vehicle can be broken into planes, proportions, and assemblies inside a CAD workflow. It was a study in restraint as much as detail.

Process

The workflow moved from reference study to blockout, surface definition, feature separation, and final presentation views. Proportion and edge control stayed central the entire time.

Outcome

The CAD model reinforced the value of clean reference relationships and disciplined modeling choices. It improved how I plan a surface before I begin building it.
